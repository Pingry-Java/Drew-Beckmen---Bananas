The man rode into the sunset desperately searching for answers and pondering lifes meaning.
Why was life treating Sringeri so poorly? God only knows? Or does he? As Sringeri crossed
the border into the big city, he was taken back by the pungent smell of the plethora of spices
populating the markets lining the city streets. Distracted by these foreign smells, Sringeri
didn't not realize that a deluge had begun. The rain, polluted by India's smog-filled air, burned through 
the banana peals and destroyed his best harvest ever. 
Traumatized at the bitter reality of no income for the season, Sringeri stabbed himself to death with a fruit knife, the banana juice mixing with his blood in the open wound
